Why continuous data matters

Knowing hormone levels in real time enables dynamic dosing of HRT to smooth out short‑term fluctuations, aligning treatment to how you actually feel day to day.

  • Reduce time spent on trial‑and‑error dose changes
  • Lower avoidable side effects by adjusting earlier
  • Support diagnostic decisions with objective trends

Aggregated, privacy‑preserving data can accelerate research—for example, exploring whether the estrogen‑to‑progesterone ratio affects long‑term endometrial cancer risk.

Why single blood tests fall short: Hormone levels can swing widely from day to day during perimenopause. A one‑off blood draw is a snapshot that often misses the true pattern—trends over time are what matter.

Technology

The EndoSense continuous hormone monitor (CHM) uses a robust recognition technology, capable of being regenerated by a small electrical current. This overcomes challenges with stability, regeneration and individual calibration that aptamer-based technologies face.
